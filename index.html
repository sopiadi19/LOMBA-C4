About tea

Tea (Camellia Sinensis) is a green leafy shrub used as an ingredient in tea, which is one of the world's most popular healthy beverages and second only to water. The tea plant is thought to have originated in the mountainous regions of the Himalayas and the mountainous regions bordering the People's Republic of China, India and Burma.



history of tea

The history of tea began in ancient China around 2737 BC, when Emperor Shen Nong discovered the drink by accident. Tea is an important drink in Chinese culture, used in ceremonies and traditional medicine. In the 8th century AD, tea spread to Japan, Korea and the rest of the world. In Japan, tea is the center of the tea ceremony which has deep philosophical values. The tea trade brought the drink to Europe and North America, where it became popular across all levels of society. Tea remains a very popular drink throughout the world, with many varieties such as black, green, white, oolong and herbal teas.

types of tea

01

Jasmine Tea
Jasmine tea is one of the most widely consumed types of tea in Indonesia. Jasmine tea is known for its calming aroma. This beverage has been around in China since 700 years ago. High-quality loose tea leaves are blended with jasmine petals, giving the tea a delicate, slightly sweet flavor and aromatic scent.

02

Green Tea
Green tea is tea derived from the dried leaves of the camellia sinesis plant. Green tea is rich in antioxidan content in the form of polyphenols that can ward off free radicals that are not good for the body. These polyphenols also give green tea its distinctive, slightly bitter flavor.

03

Black Tea
Black tea has a myriad of health benefits. The flavor of black tea is stronger than green tea. Black tea is derived from Camellia Sinesis tea leaves. Its color is more intense due to the oxidation process which results in a higher caffeine content, which is about 40 mg per cup.

04

Oolong Tea
Oolong tea is similar to black tea. The fermentation process is very short, which gives this tea a strong flavor. Oolong tea comes from the leaves of the camellia sinensis plant. In China, oolong tea is known as wulong tea and has been consumed for centuries. Like black tea, oolong tea is produced through a process of oxidation. Oolong leaves wither after being harvested and go through a short-term drying process.

05

Chamomile Tea
Chamomile tea comes from the petals of chamomile flowers as its main ingredient. This tea is known for its relaxing properties and for helping us sleep better.

06

Rose Tea
Rose tea is known to relieve menstrual pain. In addition, rose tea is also rich in vitamin C which can help with skin care, acne, hair health, and immunity. Rose tea can also improve digestive tract health as it stimulates the body's production of bile, which helps the stomach digest food efficiently. As with other types of tea, rose tea also has a calming, therapeutic effect.

07

White Tea
White tea comes from the same tea leaves as black tea and green tea, but white tea is made from young tea leaves that are dried without being oxidized, resulting in a unique flavor and aroma.

08

Turmeric Tea
Not only can it be used as herbal medicine, turmeric can also be used as a tea that is rich in benefits. Turmeric is known to have the best anti-aging, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory properties. If consumed as tea, you will get the benefits of turmeric that warms and makes the body fresher.

09

Ginger Tea
Ginger tea is known as a refined drink that can warm the body. No doubt, ginger is often an option when the weather or climate is cold. After drinking ginger tea, the body will feel warmer and more comfortable.

10

Peppermint Tea
Peppermint tea is made from a mixture of tea leaves with peppermint leaves. This tea is quite famous as a cure for sinus disease because it has a fresh sensation that can smooth the respiratory tract and refresh the throat.

11

Herbal Tea
Herbal teas are among the only types of tea beverages that do not contain leaves from the camellia plant. Technically, herbal teas are made from leaves, roots, seeds, bark, and flowers that have medicinal properties.

12

Moringa Tea
As the name implies, moringa tea is a type of tea made from moringa leaves, aka moringa leaves. All components of this plant are edible, from flowers, roots, seeds, to leaves. Moringa tea is made from dried and powdered leaves. Moringa tea has a mild flavor with a grassy aroma. This tea contains nutrients that are rich in vitamin C, B vitamins, and antioxidants. Moringa leaves themselves are believed to be able to lower blood sugar and cholesterol levels.

13

Dandelion Tea
Dandelion tea made from dandelion roots has the same energy-boosting and concentration-boosting benefits as coffee. In addition, dandelion root tea has a flavor that is not much different from coffee. It helps to maintain digestive health, strengthen immunity, improve bone health, and reduce the risk of liver disease. There is a lot of evidence that dandelion root tea can cure liver disease.

14

krisan Tea
Chrysanthemum tea is a type of flower tea from China that is often consumed today. Unlike regular tea, chrysanthemum tea does not contain caffeine as it is not derived from Camellia sinensis tea leaves. Chrysanthemum tea is commonly used to reduce fever and early cold symptoms.

15

Yerba Mate Tea
Yerba mate tea is an herbal drink brewed from the leaves of the mate tree. The mate tree can only be found growing in the rainforests of Argentina, Chile, Peru, Brazil, Uruguay, and Paraguay - and is commonly used as a medicinal raw material.
